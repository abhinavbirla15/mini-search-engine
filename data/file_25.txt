Exploring Virtual RealityVirtual reality (VR) immerses users in simulated environments using headsets like Oculus. Applications span gaming, training, and therapy. Architects visualize buildings, surgeons practice procedures.Challenges include motion sickness and high costs. Augmented reality (AR) overlays digital elements on reality, as in Pokémon GO. Advancements in haptic feedback enhance realism.VR transforms education and remote work. Ethical concerns involve addiction and privacy. As technology matures, VR promises limitless possibilities.
