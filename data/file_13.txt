The Psychology of MotivationMotivation drives human behavior, explained by theories like Maslow's hierarchy of needs, which prioritizes physiological, safety, love, esteem, and self-actualization. Intrinsic motivation comes from internal satisfaction, while extrinsic relies on rewards.Daniel Pink's framework emphasizes autonomy, mastery, and purpose. Dopamine plays a key role in reward-seeking. Procrastination often stems from fear or lack of clarity.Strategies include setting SMART goals and building habits. Understanding motivation enhances productivity in work, education, and personal growth. Cultivating it leads to fulfillment and achievement.
